As single-junction perovskite solar cells approach silicon parity, the perovskite/transport-layer interface has become the central bottleneck for both efficiency and operating stability. This review classifies and benchmarks three complementary synthetic strategies that target this bottleneck — multifunctional molecular passivation that combines Lewis acids, bases and ionic groups to heal defects and repel minority carriers; heterostructure interlayers (2D perovskites, perovskitoids and unrelated materials, inserted as bilayers or graded layers) that modulate band alignment and block degradation pathways; and self-assembled monolayers whose modular anchor, linker and head group independently tune surface energetics, passivation and crystallization. Frontline reports exceeding 26% PCE and 2000 h MPP T90 are tabulated alongside the synthesis–processing relationships needed to extend these strategies to narrow- and wide-bandgap sub-cells in tandems and to scalable blade- and slot-die deposition.
