A sulfur-modified methylthio molecule provides chemical defect passivation through strong coordination and hydrogen bonding, while a diammonium molecule repels minority carriers via field-effect passivation, together yielding a certified 25.1% quasi-steady-state PCE and stable operation at 65 &deg;C for &gt;2000 h in ambient air.
